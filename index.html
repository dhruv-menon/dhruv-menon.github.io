Research

My overarching goal is to harness the power of computation for the large-scale discovery of materials that benefit humanity.
I aim to design versatile materials for applications ranging from drug delivery and gas separation to catalysis.

Generative materials design.

The molecular landscape is intractable to experimental approaches. Learning and effectively navigating the chemical space offers the possibility to design materials with excellent performance and desirable properties.

Drug delivery.

In cancer therapy, drug accessibility to the tumour site is very poor. Chemical space navigation facilitates the design of materials that can effectively access and deliver drugs to tumours.

Gas storage and separation.

Separation of similarly-sized gases is critical, but very challenging. Designing materials that can selectively capture and separate gases can significantly advance sustainability efforts.
